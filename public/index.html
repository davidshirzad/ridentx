North Texas Cycling Community
Road / Trail
White Rock Lake Loop
9.4 mi · Easy · East Dallas
Dallas's most beloved cycling loop. Hugs the shoreline with a stunning downtown skyline view on the east side. Best early morning before pedestrian traffic peaks. Combine with White Rock Creek Trail for a 24-mile out.
AllTrails →
Road / Trail
Trinity Trails — Fort Worth
40+ mi · Easy–Moderate · Fort Worth
Fort Worth's premier trail network along the Trinity River, connecting 21 parks including the Botanic Garden, Stockyards, and downtown. Outstanding for long steady-state efforts and a genuinely beautiful urban ride.
AllTrails →
Road / Trail
Campion Trail — Irving
13 mi · Easy · Irving / Las Colinas
Follows the Elm Fork of the Trinity River through Irving — one of the longest continuous trail segments in the area. Connects to other major systems for a true long-day out-and-back option west of Dallas.
AllTrails →
Road / Trail
Santa Fe Trail
4.5 mi · Easy · East Dallas
Connects White Rock Lake to Deep Ellum and Fair Park along a former railroad corridor. Best paired with the lake loop for a ride that ends at a great post-ride destination. Shaded and well-maintained.
AllTrails →
MTB
DORBA Trails — Cedar Hill SP
23 mi · Difficult · Cedar Hill
The premier North Texas mountain bike experience. DORBA-maintained singletrack within Cedar Hill State Park. Technical terrain with meaningful elevation change. Requires day-use park fee. Worth every cent.
DORBA Info →
MTB
Northshore Trail — Lake Grapevine
23 mi · Moderate–Difficult · Flower Mound
Seven interconnected loops at Lake Grapevine with dramatic lakeside cliff views. A favorite for intermediate to advanced riders. Park centrally at MADD Shelter for access to all loops. One of the best MTB experiences in DFW.
AllTrails →
Gravel
Denton County Farm Roads
40–100 mi · Moderate · Denton / Celina / Sherman
The Denton–Celina–Gunter–Sherman corridor offers rolling gravel with low traffic and excellent road surfaces. Build your own routes on RideWithGPS or Komoot. Increasingly the heart of the North Texas gravel scene.
Plan on RideWithGPS →
Gravel
Palo Pinto / Mineral Wells Roads
40–120 mi · Moderate–Hard · West of DFW
Rugged West Texas character just 90 minutes from Dallas. Rolling caliche and chip-seal roads through ranch country with real climbing. Increasingly popular as the North Texas gravel community expands its range.
Plan on RideWithGPS →
