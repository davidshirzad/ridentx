Hugs the shoreline with a stunning downtown skyline view on the east side. Best early morning before pedestrian traffic peaks. Combine with White Rock Creek Trail for a 24-mile out. AllTrails → ROAD / TRAIL Katy Trail 3.5 mi · Easy · Uptown Dallas Built on the historic Missouri-Kansas-Texas Railroad corridor, connecting Knox-Henderson, Uptown, and Victory Park. Ideal warm-up or recovery ride. University Crossing extends it east toward White Rock Lake via the SMU campus edge. AllTrails → ROAD / TRAIL Trinity Trails — Fort Worth 40+ mi · Easy–Moderate · Fort Worth Fort Worth's premier trail network along the Trinity River, connecting 21 parks including the Botanic Garden, Stockyards, and downtown. Outstanding for long steady-state efforts and a genuinely beautiful urban ride. AllTrails → ROAD / TRAIL Campion Trail — Irving 13 mi · Easy · Irving / Las Colinas Follows the Elm Fork of the Trinity River through Irving — one of the longest continuous trail segments in the area. Connects to other major systems for a true long-day out-and-back option west of Dallas. AllTrails → ROAD / TRAIL Santa Fe Trail 4.5 mi · Easy · East Dallas Connects White Rock Lake to Deep Ellum and Fair Park along a former railroad corridor. Best paired with the lake loop for a ride that ends at a great post-ride destination. Shaded and well-maintained. AllTrails → MTB DORBA Trails — Cedar Hill SP 23 mi · Difficult · Cedar Hill The premier North Texas mountain bike experience. DORBA-maintained singletrack within Cedar Hill State Park. Technical terrain with meaningful elevation change. Requires day-use park fee. Worth every cent. DORBA Info → MTB Northshore Trail — Lake Grapevine 23 mi · Moderate–Difficult · Flower Mound Seven interconnected loops at Lake Grapevine with dramatic lakeside cliff views. A favorite for intermediate to advanced riders. Park centrally at MADD Shelter for access to all loops. One of the best MTB experiences in DFW. AllTrails → GRAVEL Denton County Farm Roads 40–100 mi · Moderate · Denton / Celina / Sherman The Denton–Celina–Gunter–Sherman corridor offers rolling gravel with low traffic and excellent road surfaces. Build your own routes on RideWithGPS or Komoot. Increasingly the heart of the North Texas gravel scene.
